# Pricing Strategy — Examples

## Example 1: Design SaaS Pricing Tiers

### Prompt
> Design pricing tiers for our project management tool. We currently charge $15/user/month flat. Competitors range from free to $25/user.

### What the skill does
1. Analyzes competitive landscape and identifies positioning gap
2. Designs 3-tier structure: Free (up to 3 users, 5 projects), Pro ($12/user/month, unlimited), Business ($20/user/month, admin controls + integrations)
3. Recommends annual pricing with 20% discount
4. Suggests value metric shift from per-seat to per-workspace for small teams
5. Designs pricing page layout with Pro as highlighted "Most Popular" tier

---

## Example 2: Competitive Pricing Analysis

### Prompt
> Analyze competitor pricing in the email marketing space. We are launching a new product and need to position our pricing.

### What the skill does
1. Maps 8 competitors on price-vs-features matrix
2. Normalizes pricing to "cost per 10K contacts/month" for comparison
3. Identifies underserved segment: mid-market ($200-400/month range has few options)
4. Recommends penetration pricing at $249/month for 25K contacts to capture gap

## Example 3: Pricing Page Optimization

### Prompt
> Our pricing page gets good traffic (2K/month) but only 3% click through to signup. Help us improve.

### What the skill does
1. Audits current page: 5 plans (too many), no recommended plan highlighted, 35-row feature table, monthly-only billing
2. Recommends: 3 plans, highlight middle as "Most Popular", 10-row feature table, add annual toggle with savings badge
3. Adds social proof per tier and FAQ section addressing top 5 pricing objections

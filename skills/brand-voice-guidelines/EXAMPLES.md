# Brand Voice & Guidelines — Examples

## Example 1: Develop Voice from Scratch
### Prompt
> Develop brand voice guidelines for our fintech startup. We help small businesses manage cash flow. Our audience is non-technical small business owners who are frustrated with complex financial tools.

### What the skill does
1. Recommends archetype blend: Caregiver (supportive) + Sage (knowledgeable) with Everyperson (relatable)
2. Defines 4 voice attributes: Approachable (not corporate), Confident (not arrogant), Clear (not oversimplified), Empathetic (not patronizing)
3. Creates tone matrix for 6 contexts (marketing, support, onboarding, error messages, social, email)
4. Builds messaging framework: brand promise, 3 value propositions with proof points, elevator pitch, tagline options
5. Writes vocabulary guide: forbidden jargon list, preferred alternatives
6. Produces complete Brand Voice Document with before/after examples

## Example 2: Voice Consistency Audit
### Prompt
> Audit our brand voice across website, email, and social. Our blog sounds casual and fun, but our product pages sound corporate. Help us fix the inconsistency.

### What the skill does
1. Samples 3 pages per channel, scores on 4 voice attributes (1-5 each)
2. Identifies gap: blog averages 4.5/5 on "Approachable" but product pages average 2/5
3. Finds root cause: product pages were written by a different team using formal templates
4. Rewrites 3 product page sections to match the blog voice
5. Creates a voice checklist for the product team to self-audit future pages

## Example 3: Tone Adaptation for Crisis
### Prompt
> We had a data breach. Help us write communications in our brand voice but adapted for crisis — we usually sound playful and casual.

### What the skill does
1. References tone matrix: Crisis context = high confidence, high formality, zero humor, very high empathy
2. Shows how to keep brand voice (honest, direct) while shifting tone (serious, accountable)
3. Writes 3 communications: customer email, social post, press statement
4. Each maintains brand identity (still sounds like the brand) while being appropriately serious
5. Provides anti-examples: what NOT to say in a crisis with their brand voice

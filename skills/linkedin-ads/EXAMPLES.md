# LinkedIn Ads — Examples

## Example 1: B2B SaaS Campaign Setup

### Prompt

> Set up LinkedIn ad campaigns for our B2B analytics platform targeting VP+ marketing leaders at mid-market SaaS companies. Budget: $5,000/month. Goal: demo requests.

### What the skill does

1. Recommends campaign structure: 2 campaign groups (Awareness + Lead Gen)
2. Builds targeting: Job Function: Marketing + Seniority: VP/Director/C-Suite + Company Size: 201-1000 + Industry: Computer Software
3. Recommends Lead Gen Forms with 3 fields (name, email, company — pre-filled)
4. Suggests creative mix: 2 Document Ads (gated report), 2 Sponsored Content (case study), 1 Video (product demo)
5. Sets budget split: 60% Lead Gen, 40% Awareness
6. Provides CPA targets based on benchmarks

## Example 2: ABM Campaign

### Prompt

> We have a target account list of 200 companies. Help me build an ABM campaign on LinkedIn.

### What the skill does

1. Uploads company list as Matched Audience (validates 300+ match minimum — may need to expand)
2. Layers seniority targeting (Director+) on top of company list
3. Recommends 3-phase approach: Awareness (Sponsored Content) → Consideration (Document Ads) → Conversion (Message Ads)
4. Sets frequency caps to avoid ad fatigue on small audience
5. Suggests engagement retargeting between phases

## Example 3: Campaign Audit

### Prompt

> Audit our LinkedIn Ads account. We are spending $8K/month but only getting 12 leads. CPL is $667 which is way too high.

### What the skill does

1. Reviews account structure: finds 6 campaigns all targeting overlapping audiences
2. Identifies audience overlap causing internal bid competition
3. Finds targeting is too narrow (8,000 audience size — below 50K minimum)
4. Notes Lead Gen Forms have 7 fields (reducing completion rate to 6%)
5. Recommends: consolidate to 2 campaigns, broaden audience to 150K, reduce form to 3 fields
6. Estimates CPL reduction to $150-200 range
